Support

Help us reach more students.

EnvironMentors runs on real resources: research supplies, science-fair costs, travel, and the time it takes to run the program well. Funding is what lets us take on more students. There are two ways to help.

Donate

Supporting EnvironMentors is a direct way for community leaders, local businesses, and individual donors to show their commitment to our community, our environment, and the next generation of students here.

It's a double impact. Your gift backs two groups at once: the high school students taking their first real steps in science, and the UC Davis graduate students who mentor them. Few programs give graduate students mentorship experience this hands-on, the kind they carry forward for the rest of their careers. A gift to EnvironMentors invests in rising high schoolers and in UC Davis students at the same time, and in research that matters for our community and the world.

Local businesses have sponsored our science fair, field trips, student travel, and student awards. Individual donors make a real difference too, and every dollar goes directly to supporting students.

We can't grow and reach more students without your help. To give, get in touch with Grey Monroe or the UC Davis Development Office. A dedicated donation page is coming soon, and donations of lab surplus and equipment are welcome too.

For UC Davis PIs: broader impacts, handled

EnvironMentors is a strong, ready-made broader-impacts activity for your research proposals. You bring the science; we provide the platform, the administration, and the structure around it. Local high school students do real research connected to work happening at UC Davis.

If you'd like to include EnvironMentors in your broader impacts, let us know and we're happy to write a letter of support. And if you can, please consider a small line item in your budget to help sustain the program: about $1,000 supports one student for a full year. Contributions like that are what let us work together and keep the program running.
